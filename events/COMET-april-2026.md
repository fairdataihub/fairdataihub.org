## 📝 Event Description

The **COMET (Collaborative Metadata Enrichment Taskforce) Community Meeting** brings together researchers, infrastructure developers, and open science advocates to advance community-led metadata enrichment using trusted, persistent identifiers. During the April meeting, two developments will be presented that, together, illustrate the COMET model in practice.

The first presentation introduces **posters.science**, a free and open-source platform being developed by our team to extract rich, structured metadata from scientific posters to make them FAIR and AI-ready. COMET will collaborate on this project to bring enriched poster metadata into DataCite DOI records, building on the recent release of **DataCite Metadata Schema version 4.7**, which introduces "poster" as a supported resource type.

The second presentation features the **DataCite product team** walking through DataCite's new enrichment layer with a live example, covering its data model, provenance values, additive design, and available API endpoints.

More information and registration are available on the [COMET website](https://www.cometadata.org/). Register directly via the [Zoom registration link](https://datacite.zoom.us/meeting/register/B5KGjV5QQQO2-p8WtSNzQw#/registration).

## 💡 Our Role / Contribution

**Bhavesh Patel** will co-present with **Jamey O'Neill** a talk introducing **[posters.science](https://posters.science)**, a platform designed to address the inconsistencies in how scientific poster metadata is captured, improving their discoverability and attribution by making them FAIR and AI-ready.
